New THT site redesign is now live

Another blog site redesign?

Yes siree, you bet it is.

We redesigned The Helpful Tipper blog for the third time because I like change.

Change is good and change is necessary when you work in the perpetually changing landscape of tech.

Being ready to embrace new ideas, a new look, and quite a few new bugs is what design and development are all about.

Blog site redesign objectives

We didn’t head into this redesign cycle without a plan ��

No, we came prepared which is something you should always do so you don’t lose sight of your goals.

Redesigning a website isn’t an “easy” thing.

There are so many different aspects and elements to consider so a plan is downright a necessity to avoid overwhelm and intimidation.

This round of redesign was inspired by three objectives:

1) Solidify THT’s theme and brand

I admit that the only thing we knew for sure about THT was its name and purpose (i.e. learning how to code).

Other than those two aspects, we had no concept in mind, no logo or theme, or color palette ����

Ever since we launched The Helpful Tipper those details were slowly filled in based on preferences and inspiration.

During this redesign, I broke DM’s heart (just a little) by retiring our astronaut mascot.

We are not “exploring the digital universe” any longer because we are learning how to move through it first.

As the goal of THT solidified into a blog where we share our coding journey and knowledge, we realized our claims required simplification.

So here we are, one astronaut short but 100% certain of our mission, your needs, and the brand that is THT.

2) Create opportunities for more line drawings

There’s one element I didn’t retire and that’s the line drawings.

I’m the main artist behind them so please be kind in your comments.

I consider them with a pride that is bound to make DM’s eyes roll right out of their sockets if he keeps on rolling them so.

Whatever thoughts we might have on the matter of line drawings, they’re a unique aspect of THT.

They add a personal touch and make our relationship more intimate, no?

After all, I take considerable time making them so we can give you a better glimpse at our lives ��

Coding might be technical but what newbies in the world of tech don’t realize until fully immersed is that we spend lots of time cooperating.

Building digital products, writing code, debugging, testing, and designing are all collaborative activities that require teamwork!

You and THT aren’t just a person reading from a screen - we’re here to support each other so we can be the best versions of ourselves one tag at a time ��

Playful young man and woman lineart. We collaborate in the most interesting of ways.

3) Build an email subscription list

Building a list out of your readers is something any of my bloggers out there just “get”.

It’s nice if y’all follow us on our socials (please do, we’re spunky!) but if one of those platforms goes kaput one day, we absolutely don’t want to sink with the ship ��

When you give us the pleasure of sending you our blog updates and spontaneously fabulous communications (now and then, I promise we don’t spam), we’ll never lose you.

And we don’t want to lose you because we’ve such a long, happy journey ahead of us.

So that’s the long-winded explanation of why we took extra care to choose an email provider that won’t drive me crazy.

We ditched Mailchimp and found a new email provider

We started off with Mailchimp because it’s so popular in the blogging world.

I’m afraid my developer brain never got around to their dashboard UI. I couldn’t make heads or tails of their system even though it looks all pretty and sensible.

At the end of the day, Mailchimp wasn’t intuitive in the least and that made me dread doing anything related to email lists.

It took a single Google search to realize there were plenty of other email providers we could try, one of which is Mailerlite, our current provider.
